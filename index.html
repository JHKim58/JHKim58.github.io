How do plants reorganize chromatin in response to changing environments?

Our lab studies how environmental signals reshape chromatin architecture, epigenetic states, and genome organization to regulate plant growth, development, and adaptation.

Explore Research Join the Lab

Research Themes

Plant epigenetics · Chromatin dynamics · 3D genome organization · Environmental adaptation · Climate resilience

Model system: Arabidopsis for mechanistic discovery, with long-term expansion to crop and horticultural species relevant to food security.

Research

We investigate how plants integrate environmental signals through epigenetic and chromatin-based mechanisms. By combining genetics, genomics, chromatin profiling, and genome architecture approaches, we aim to uncover how nuclear organization shapes adaptive developmental responses.

Environmental chromatin remodeling

We study how light, temperature, and other environmental cues trigger changes in chromatin accessibility, histone modification, and transcriptional regulation.

3D genome architecture and chromatin looping

We investigate how chromatin loops and higher-order genome organization contribute to plant development and environmental adaptation.

Epigenome dynamics and climate resilience

Our long-term goal is to translate mechanistic discoveries from Arabidopsis to crop and horticultural species relevant to controlled-environment agriculture and food security.
